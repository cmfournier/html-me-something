Carlos Fournier: My Professional Journey

Sun-Sentinel

My first professional job was working as an agate clerk covering high school sports for the South Florida Sun-Sentinel. This was a fun job that primarily combined data entry with writing and editing.

CTI Consulting

This was a very short-lived job, but it was significant because I was working as a technical recruiter. This exposed me to the world of programming, development, and database technologies.

Mercy Home for Boys & Girls

This is where my professional interest in more technical pursuits began to take shape. I started out managing a data entry team on an AS400 database. Eventually I helped oversee a database migration to a web-based CRM system and became more interested in how databases work.

Food for the Poor

This is where I currently work. I analyze a lot of transactional data from our database using SQL, and I also select and extract large datasets to send direct mailings to members of our database. When I started working here, we were again using an AS400 database. However, as of last year, we have migrated to a CRM platform. I've had to learn the hard way that different databases and CRMs can be structured much differently, and that the SQL needed to traverse the tables can be far different from one database to another.
